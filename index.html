About
Bridging AI and
Clinical Neuroscience

I am a final-year PhD candidate in the AIM for Health Lab (led by A/Prof. Zongyuan Ge) at Monash University's Faculty of IT. My research sits at the intersection of computer vision, generative AI, and clinical neuroscience.

I play a key technical role in the CRC-P project "AI-powered clinical decision assistance for acute stroke" — a national multi-institution collaboration with Apollo Medical Imaging Technology, Ingham Institute, and Queensland Health. My work spans multi-centre data curation across imaging modalities (CTP, CTA, NCCT) and clinical data, developing end-to-end models for lesion segmentation, large vessel occlusion detection, and treatment outcome prediction, to be validated across 17 hospital sites.

Prior to my PhD, I completed my MSc and BSc at East China Normal University, where I worked on radiomics and deep learning for glioma diagnosis. I also interned at Siemens Healthineers, building cerebral vasculature segmentation and defect inspection systems.

Research
Research Highlights
🧠

AI-Powered Stroke Decision Support

Key technical contributor to a national CRC-P project with industry and clinical partners. Developed end-to-end pipelines for ischemic lesion segmentation, large vessel occlusion detection, and treatment outcome prediction from multi-modal imaging (CTP, CTA, NCCT) and clinical data — to be validated across 17 hospital sites.

CRC-P Multi-Centre CTP Clinical Deployment
🎨

Semantic-Guided 3D CT Synthesis

Developed a latent diffusion model for synthesizing 3D CT images with controllable stroke lesions. Designed a Spatial-Semantic Feature Interaction Module integrating DINOv2 features with adapters, improving downstream segmentation by up to 22% in Dice.

LDM DINOv2 ControlNet 3D nnU-Net
🔬

Logic-Guided Diagnostic Reasoning

Built an interpretable framework for medical image classification using logic Concept Bottleneck Models to construct Chain-of-Thought reasoning chains, fine-tuning LLMs (Qwen3-VL, LLaVA, LLaMA) via LoRA for clinically grounded diagnosis.

CBM CoT Qwen3-VL LoRA
📊

Glioma Radiomics & Deep Learning

Developed multi-task radiomics and deep learning models for predicting epilepsy and IDH mutation status in glioma patients from preoperative MRI, with shared feature representations and comprehensive preprocessing pipelines.
